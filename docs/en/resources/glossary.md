# Glossary

**Annotation**  
A structured label added to data, such as a lemma, part-of-speech tag, named entity, emotion category or editorial note. Automatic annotation is a model output and needs validation.

**API**  
Application programming interface: a documented way for software to request data or actions from another system.

**Corpus**  
A documented collection of texts assembled for analysis. A corpus has scope, composition, metadata, transformations and limits; it is not merely a folder of files.

**Data model**  
An explicit representation of the entities, attributes, relationships and constraints relevant to a project.

**Database**  
A structured system for storing and querying records. A relational database organizes records in linked tables and is commonly queried with SQL.

**Digital humanities**  
Research and teaching that combine humanistic questions and interpretation with computational, data-intensive or digitally mediated methods, while critically examining the infrastructures involved.

**DOI**  
Digital Object Identifier: a persistent identifier used for publications, datasets, software and other research objects.

**Entity resolution**  
The process of deciding which real or conceptual entity a name or mention refers to and linking it to a stable identifier.

**FAIR**  
Principles encouraging research objects to be findable, accessible, interoperable and reusable. FAIR does not mean that all data must be openly accessible.

**Gazetteer**  
A structured place-name resource, often containing identifiers, alternative names, types, coordinates, dates and administrative relationships.

**Geocoding**  
Linking an address or place string to a spatial entity or coordinate. Historical geocoding requires period-aware evidence and uncertainty.

**GIS**  
Geographic information system: tools and data structures for storing, analysing and visualizing spatial information.

**Lemma**  
A dictionary-like base form used to group inflected word forms, such as *hiša*, *hiše* and *hišo* under *hiša*.

**Metadata**  
Structured information about another object: source, creator, date, genre, language, rights, processing history, uncertainty and more.

**Model**  
A simplified formal representation used to organize, explain or predict phenomena. Models include database schemas, statistical models, annotation systems and conceptual categories.

**Named entity recognition (NER)**  
Automatic identification of spans that refer to categories such as people, organizations, places or dates. Recognition does not by itself resolve identity.

**Network**  
A model containing nodes and edges. Its meaning depends on how nodes and relationships are defined from sources.

**OCR**  
Optical character recognition: prediction of text from document images. OCR quality should be measured for the material and task in question.

**Operationalization**  
Turning a theoretical concept into observable categories, variables or procedures that can be applied to evidence.

**Provenance**  
The record of where data came from, who changed it, which transformations were applied and which evidence supports a claim.

**Release**  
A named, versioned and frozen edition of a digital project. A release can be reviewed, cited and archived even while development continues elsewhere.

**Reproducibility**  
The ability to obtain the same or acceptably equivalent result from the same data, code and documented environment.

**Sentiment analysis**  
Classification of evaluative polarity, usually toward a target. It is not equivalent to emotion, stance or psychological state.

**SQL**  
Structured Query Language, used to define, filter, join, aggregate and inspect tables in relational databases.

**Stylometry**  
Quantitative analysis of style through measurable textual features, often used to study authorship, genre, period or translation style.

**TEI**  
Text Encoding Initiative: a community and set of XML guidelines for representing scholarly, literary and historical texts.

**Topic model**  
A model that represents recurring patterns of words or document features. A topic is a model component interpreted and labelled by researchers, not a naturally discovered subject.

**Version control**  
A system, such as Git, that records changes to files and supports comparison, review, branching and restoration.
