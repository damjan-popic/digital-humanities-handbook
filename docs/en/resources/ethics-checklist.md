# Ethics and rights checklist

Use this before collecting, scraping, annotating, mapping, modelling, publishing or applying AI to cultural material. A checked box is not ethical approval; every answer should be supported by a short decision note.

## Purpose and necessity

- Is the research or teaching purpose explicit?
- Is every collected field necessary for that purpose?
- Could the question be answered with less sensitive or less identifiable data?
- Are exploratory future uses being presented honestly rather than as unlimited permission?

## People and communities

- Are living people represented directly or indirectly?
- Could linking datasets reveal family, health, political, religious, migration or identity information?
- Are minors, vulnerable people or small communities involved?
- Is consent required, possible and meaningful?
- Would anonymization actually prevent re-identification after aggregation or quotation?
- Has the represented community had a meaningful route to comment, restrict or correct?

## Sources and expectations

- Was the material created for public circulation or a limited context?
- Does technical accessibility match reasonable expectations of reuse?
- Have deleted, restricted or access-controlled materials been handled appropriately?
- Does bulk collection change the ethical character of individually visible records?

## Rights and licences

- Who holds rights in text, image, audio, video, code, annotation and data?
- What does each licence allow: analysis, quotation, redistribution, adaptation or commercial reuse?
- Are platform terms, database rights or contractual limits relevant?
- Can raw data be shared, or only metadata, derived features, identifiers or reconstruction scripts?
- Are attribution and third-party notices complete?

## Representation and bias

- What was never created, preserved, digitized, catalogued or made machine-readable?
- Which groups, languages, periods or genres are over- or underrepresented?
- Does OCR, annotation or model performance differ across relevant groups?
- Could categories repeat harmful historical classifications?
- Are missing data visibly distinguished from absence?

## AI and automation

- Is the task suitable for automation, or does it require human contextual judgement?
- Are model, version/date, prompt, parameters and supplied context recorded?
- Are generated factual claims and quotations verified against sources?
- Is there a baseline or human-labelled sample?
- Are subgroup errors and unstable outputs examined?
- Is human responsibility for final decisions named?

## Publication and governance

- Is public release necessary, or would restricted access be safer?
- Are uncertainty, limitations and model use visible to readers?
- Is there a correction, contact and takedown route?
- Are contributors credited and did they knowingly accept the licence?
- Are student assessment and consent to public authorship separated?
- Has a review date been set for software-dependent or sensitive material?
