# Review policy

## Routine contributions

Workflows, translations and small corrections require at least one substantive review by a maintainer or designated reviewer. Code-bearing contributions must include a reproducibility check on the smallest provided example.

## Stable handbook releases

A major release should receive two forms of review:

- **subject review**, covering disciplinary accuracy, interpretation and relevance;
- **method and pedagogy review**, covering technical validity, reproducibility, accessibility and suitability for learners.

For a formally published university edition, external reviews follow the publisher's required form. Review comments and responses may be archived privately when confidentiality is required, while the public release notes record the review scope.

## Translation review

A translation must be checked for disciplinary terminology, instructions that affect execution, links and cross-references, and consistency with the source version. Machine-assisted translation may be used only as a draft and must be identified until a competent human reviewer approves it.

## Review states

Pages may use one of these editorial states in front matter:

- `draft`
- `tested`
- `reviewed`
- `archived`

A numbered release manifest records the state of every included page.
